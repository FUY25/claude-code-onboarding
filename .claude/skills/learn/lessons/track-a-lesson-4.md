# Lesson 4: When Claude Gets It Wrong

## Goal
The user understands that Claude makes mistakes, knows how to spot them, and knows the two recovery moves: clarify and undo.

## Delivery instructions

Say:
> "Lesson 4 is about something that will definitely happen: Claude will get something wrong.
>
> This isn't a flaw — it's how AI works. The important thing is knowing what to do when it happens. There are two moves."

---

## Move 1: Clarify

Say:
> "The most common reason Claude gets something wrong is that your request was ambiguous. Claude made a choice — just not the one you wanted.
>
> The fix is simple: tell it what you actually wanted.
>
> Example: you asked for a 'short summary' and got three paragraphs. Just say:
>
> `That's too long. Give me a 3-sentence summary maximum.`
>
> You don't need to start over. Claude remembers the whole conversation and will adjust."

---

## Move 2: Undo

Say:
> "If Claude changed or created a file and you don't like it, you can undo it.
>
> Type:
>
> `Undo the last change you made to [filename]`
>
> Or if you're not sure which file changed:
>
> `What did you just change? I want to revert it.`
>
> Claude will tell you what it did and can restore the previous version."

---

## Activity

Say:
> "Let's practice. Ask Claude something intentionally vague:
>
> `Write something about the project.`
>
> Claude will produce something. Whatever it produces, respond with:
>
> `That's not quite what I meant. I wanted [be specific about what you actually want].`
>
> Then watch Claude adjust. That back-and-forth is the real skill."

After the exchange:
> "Lesson 4 done. You now know:
> - Claude gets things wrong — that's normal
> - Clarify: just tell it what you wanted
> - Undo: ask it to revert file changes
>
> One more lesson to go. Lesson 5 is about staying safe — what Claude can and can't do to your computer, and how to stay in control."
