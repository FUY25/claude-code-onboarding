Interview: Sarah Chen, Senior PM @ Gridwork (Series B, ~120 ppl)
Date: Feb 12 2026
Conducted by: Jamie

Background: 6 yrs product mgmt, prev at Atlassian. Manages team of 8 engineers + 2 designers.

Current tools: Jira, Confluence, Slack, Figma, Google Sheets for roadmap tracking
She mentioned "spreadsheet hell" multiple times lol

Pain points:
- Status updates take forever. "I spend Monday mornings just chasing people on Slack asking what they did last week... it's like pulling teeth"
- Jira is too heavyweight. Engineers hate filling out tickets. "They just don't update the status, so the board is always wrong"
- Roadmap lives in google sheets, gets out of sync constantly
- No single source of truth — stakeholders ask her the same questions bc they can't find info themselves
- Cross-team dependencies are a nightmare... she drew this whole diagram on the whiteboard showing how 3 teams block each other

What she wants:
- Automatic status updates somehow? She mentioned Linear does this better but hasn't tried it
- Simple views for different audiences — eng needs detail, execs need high level
- "I just want ONE place where everything lives. Not Jira for tickets, Confluence for docs, Sheets for roadmap..."
- Timeline/gantt view that actually stays updated
- Better async standups — "we tried Geekbot but it felt like homework"

Interesting quote: "The best project management tool is the one people actually use. Everything we've tried, adoption drops off after 2 months."

She got really animated about the notification problem — too many notifications from Jira/Slack, so people ignore them all, then miss important stuff

Feature requests:
- Smart notifications (only ping me when something actually needs my attention)
- Auto-generate weekly status reports from activity
- Dependencies visualization
- Customizable views per role

Would pay: "Easily $15-20/seat if it actually replaced Jira AND our spreadsheets"

Follow-up: she wants to do a workflow walkthrough session, scheduling for next week
