Interview — Lisa Yamamoto, Lead Product Designer
Company: BrightLoop (edtech, ~80 people)
Date: Feb 15 2026
Notes by Jamie — audio recording failed halfway thru so second half is rough

Background: design lead, 4 designers on her team. 8 years experience, prev at Spotify.

Tools: Figma (obv), Jira (hates it), Notion (docs), Slack, Loom for async feedback

Opening quote that sets the tone:
"Jira is where design work goes to die. I put my specs in there and no one reads them. The eng team just looks at the ticket title and starts building."

Pain points — design specific:
- Handoff is broken... she creates detailed specs in Figma, links them in Jira, engineers don't click the link
- No good way to show design progress. "My work doesn't fit into To Do / In Progress / Done"
- Design reviews get lost in Slack threads
- Can't see how design tasks connect to eng tasks... feels like parallel tracks that occasionally crash into each other
- Feedback is scattered — some in Figma comments, some in Slack, some in meetings, some in Jira comments

What she wants in a PM tool:
- Visual/spatial organization — kanban but also like... mood boards? she mentioned Miro
- Design stages: research > explore > refine > handoff > QA — not just "in progress"
- Embedded image previews — "don't make me click 3 links to see what we're building"
- Better async feedback loops, threaded discussions ON the work
- Cross-functional views showing design + eng + QA in one timeline

Her ideal workflow:
1. Brief comes in as a task with context
2. She can sketch/attach explorations directly in the tool
3. Feedback happens in-context not in Slack
4. Handoff auto-generates eng tickets with design specs attached
5. She can see eng progress without asking anyone

On simplicity vs features:
"I need it to be beautiful and intuitive. If it looks like a database I won't use it."
...which is funny bc she also wants tons of customization for design workflows
She acknowledged the contradiction when I pointed it out lol

Status updates issue (again!):
"My manager asks me every Monday what I'm working on. It's in the tool! Just look!"
Same exact complaint as Sarah and David basically

Would pay: didn't discuss pricing, she said "that's not my call" but would strongly advocate if the tool understood design workflows

Random aside — she mentioned her team tried Height and liked the vibe but it was too limited
Also mentioned she misses physical sticky notes... "there's something about moving a post-it that digital tools don't capture"
