Meeting with David Chen (CEO) — Jan 8, 2026
Re: Smart Scheduling feature for CollabHub

David opened with the competitive landscape. Clockwise just raised $45M and
their AI scheduling is getting press. He's worried we're falling behind.
"If we don't ship something by end of Q2 we lose the narrative."

Revenue pressure:
- Board wants 40% YoY growth, we're tracking at 31%
- Smart scheduling could be the upsell hook for Pro tier ($29/mo vs $19/mo)
- He threw out a number: $3.2M incremental ARR if we convert 15% of free users
- "Scheduling is the wedge. Calendar is where people live."

Timeline concerns:
- Wants public beta by April 15, GA by June 1
- I pushed back — said eng estimates are 16-20 weeks minimum
- He said "find a way to cut scope, not timeline"
- Suggested we skip the Google Calendar integration at first? Just Outlook?
  I don't think that flies with our user base — 64% are on Google Workspace

Competitive notes he mentioned:
- Clockwise: AI-driven, but only does focus time blocking
- Reclaim.ai: good at habits but clunky UX
- Cal.com: open source angle, developer-focused
- "None of them do team-level scheduling well. That's our gap."

Product direction:
- Wants smart scheduling tied into our existing channels/messaging
- "Don't build a calendar app. Build scheduling intelligence inside CollabHub."
- Mentioned the idea of "meeting cost calculator" — show teams how much
  time they spend in meetings, then offer to optimize
- AI should learn from user behavior, not just rules

Other notes:
- Doesn't want a settings-heavy experience, should "just work"
- Asked about data privacy implications — GDPR, SOC2
- Wants a customer advisory board for this feature, 8-10 accounts
- Said he'd approve 3 additional headcount if we commit to April beta
