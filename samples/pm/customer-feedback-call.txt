Customer Call — Rachel Torres, VP Operations at Meridian Health
Date: Jan 12, 2026
CollabHub Enterprise account, 340 seats, renewed last quarter

Current scheduling pain points:
- "We spend more time scheduling the meeting than having the meeting"
- Her team of 28 PMs coordinates across 4 time zones (NYC, London, Singapore, Denver)
- They use CollabHub for messaging but switch to Outlook for calendar
- "Every time someone suggests a meeting time in a channel, it's a
  20-message thread of 'that doesn't work for me'"

What they do now (workarounds):
- One PM manually checks calendars and proposes times — "it's basically
  her full-time job on Wednesdays"
- They built a shared spreadsheet of "preferred meeting hours" by timezone
- Rachel personally blocks 8-10am as no-meeting time but people ignore it
- Use Doodle polls for cross-team meetings, "which is embarrassing for
  a company paying for CollabHub Enterprise"

Feature wishes:
- "I want to type 'schedule a 30-min sync with the APAC leads this week'
   in a channel and have it just happen"
- Needs to respect focus time, lunch blocks, existing commitments
- Wants to see a team-level view: "show me when my whole team is free"
- Recurring meeting optimization: "half our recurring meetings should be
  async updates, can you help us figure out which ones?"
- "Meeting prep is the other killer — can you pull relevant docs and
  previous notes automatically?"

Concerns:
- "If the AI books meetings without asking, people will revolt"
- Wants approval flow: suggest times, let people confirm
- Privacy: "Managers shouldn't see what's on my personal calendar,
  just that I'm busy"
- "We tried Clockwise and people hated it because it moved their
  meetings without warning"

Would pay more?
- "If this actually works, yes. We'd pay $35/seat easily."
- "But it has to work with Google Calendar. Half our acquired teams
  are on Google Workspace. Non-negotiable."
- Said she'd be on a customer advisory board "in a heartbeat"
