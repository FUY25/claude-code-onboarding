NovaBridge — business plan braindump
last updated feb 28 but also some stuff from the march board call

== the idea ==
AI-powered contract review platform for mid-market legal teams (10-50 attorneys)
think "Ironclad meets ChatGPT" — not original I know but the execution matters
target ACV: $48K-$120K depending on seat count

market size notes:
- legal tech market ~$29B by 2027 (Grand View Research report, need to verify)
- contract management specifically is $2.9B (2025) growing at 13.4% CAGR
- mid-market is underserved — enterprise has Ironclad/Agiloft, SMB has PandaDoc
  nobody is doing AI-native for the 10-50 attorney segment
- TAM: ~12,000 firms in US alone in this segment, so $576M-$1.44B depending on pricing
- but realistically SAM is more like $180M, need to refine this

competitors and why we win:
- Ironclad: too expensive, enterprise-focused, AI bolted on not native
- Kira Systems (acquired by Litera): good ML but legacy architecture
- LawGeex: pivoted to enterprise, left mid-market gap
- Harvey: going after BigLaw, different buyer persona entirely
- Robin AI: UK-focused, limited US presence
- SpotDraft: closest competitor honestly. but their AI accuracy is ~78% on non-standard clauses
  we're hitting 91% in our prototype (need to validate on larger dataset)

revenue model:
platform fee: $2K/mo base + $200/seat/mo
usage: $0.50 per contract reviewed (first 500 included)
enterprise add-on for API access: $1K/mo
professional services for onboarding: $15K one-time

so a 25-person firm would be: $2K + $5K + maybe $500 overage = $7.5K/mo = $90K ACV
   not bad

projection (aggressive but defensible?):
Year 1: 15 customers, $960K ARR, burn $2.4M
Year 2: 65 customers, $4.8M ARR, burn $3.1M (should be close to breakeven on unit economics)
Year 3: 180 customers, $14.4M ARR, maybe cash flow positive??
